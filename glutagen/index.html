Innovation in gluten science


The problem with undigested gluten

“Zero gluten is aspirational but not achievable”

— J. Silvester, International Symposium of CeD, Columbia University, 2018.

For many, consuming wheat triggers health issues due to indigestible gluten components. These substances can initiate harmful reactions, resulting in a range of gluten-related disorders and impacting well-being. A strict gluten free diet is the present solution, but it brings challenges including accidental gluten exposure and adherence difficulties.

Learn more



Addressing gluten challenges with natural enzymes

At Glutagen, we’ve pioneered a life-changing enzyme therapy to combat gluten-related health complications. By harnessing the Caricain enzyme found in unripe Carica Papaya fruit, this innovative approach works to break down harmful gluten peptides before they can trigger adverse reactions in the body, offering a new layer of protection in managing gluten sensitivities.
